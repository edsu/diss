In 1991 Hedstrom outlined a framework for research in digital archives which comes from the discipline history of technology and centers a sociotechnical approach that doesn't privelege social or technical explanations of how digital archives function. Critical Discourse Analysis

John Paul Gee's 7 Building Tasks.

  • Significance: foregrounding and backgrounding
  • Activities: enact spacific activities
  • Identity: positioning and recognizing identities
  • Relationships: tracing relationships between people and things
  • Politics: encoding value and norms
  • Connections: disconnecting ideas, activities, objects
  • Sign systems and knowledge: ways of knowing and believing

Inductive Thematic Analysis

Identifying patterns of meaning across a dataset.

  • Familiarization: reading, immersing
  • Coding: labeling data to identify features
  • Generating initial themes: grouping codes into themes
  • Reviewing themes: merge, split, discard themes
  • Defining and naming: analyze each theme
  • Writing: contextualize themes with existing research

These steps often loop back on each other, so keeping a journal that details reflections about the process are essential for understanding how the researchers own ideas change as a result of interacting with the data.

Trace Ethnography

Geiger and Ribes (2011)

  1. Documentary traces abound in today’s technological systems.
  2. Documentary traces are the primary mechanism in which users themselves know their distributed communities and act within them.

Modern workplaces are often distributed in space and time. This distribution is made possible by a variety of information technologies such as wikis, ticketing systems, chat systems and other types of groupware that provide version controlled and coded histories of work activity.
